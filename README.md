# Evolution of Massive Stars: Metallicity, Stellar Winds & Rotation
**Author:** Francesco Guerriero  
**Supervisors:** Prof. Marigo
**Institution:** Padua University, Astronomy Department  
**Degree:** BSc in Astronomy | Academic Year 2022/2023  

## Project Overview
The project, a literature review on massive stellar evolution, examines the key aspects of the hydrostatic evolution of
(single) massive stars, focusing on the effects of metallicity, stellar winds, and rotation. The work systematically compares stellar
models across a grid of metallicities (**[Fe/H] = 0, −1, −2, −3**), initial rotation velocities (**v = 0, 150, 300 km/s**), and stellar wind efficiency factors (**η = [0.1, 1.0]**), based primarily on the model grids of [_Limongi (2017)_](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.1706.01913)_ and [_Woosley, Heger & Weaver (2002)_](https://doi.org/10.1103/RevModPhys.74.1015). It is also discussed how the three processes, as well as their interactions, influence the ultimate fate of massive stars. 

## Structure
- **Chapter 1**: Massive star evolution: hydrostatic equilibrium, mass loss, neutrino losses,
  nuclear burning phases (C, Ne, O, Si), and the pre-supernova onion-shell structure.
- **Chapter 2**: Effects of metallicity and rotation (H/He burning, HR diagrams,
  rotation-driven mixing, WR phase); stellar winds (line-driven and dust-driven transport,
  hot/cool/WR phases, η parameter).
- **Chapter 3**: Conclusions: Fe core collapse, core bounce, neutron star/black hole formation,
  full parameter interaction, gravitational wave source compatibility.
- **Appendix A**: P Cygni profiles: qualitative and quantitative treatment, Sobolev
  approximation, mass-loss rate derivation from line profiles.
  
## Key Results
- **Metallicity**: M_WR_min increases as metallicity decreases; at [Fe/H]<−1 all stars
  explode as SNe IIP regardless of initial mass. High-mass, low-metallicity models produce
  remnants consistent with the GW150914 and GW151226 gravitational wave events.
- **Rotation**: Systematically lowers all critical mass thresholds (M_WR_min, M_NS-BH,
  M_min, PISN), promotes WR evolution, and increases He core mass through rotation-driven mixing.
- **Stellar Winds**: The efficiency parameter η dominates uncertainties across all models,
  introducing up to ~50% uncertainty in pre-supernova mass. Wind stripping of the H/He
  envelope directly determines the resulting supernova class (IIP → IIb → Ib → Ic).
